Reliability of Solar Energy

in

South East Asia

Target Audience

The users who will benefit from this visualization are energy enthusiasts, climate scientists, climate activists and businesspeople from the energy sector.

Potential tasks

The audience of this visualization will want to observe which Southeast Asia country has a better prospect of utilizing and generating a healthy amount of energy from solar compared to other alternatives in the space of renewable energy.

Importance of the project

The importance of this visualization project is to give insight to whether solar energy is a viable source of energy and should remain as an option for renewable energy.

Renewable energy consumption (% of total final energy consumption) in South East Asia

The purpose of the line chart is to visualize how much of a priority renewable enery is for the general public in South East Asian regions. The lowest among the Asian countries include Singapore, Malaysia and Japan as these countries rely more on coal powered generators while Vietna, Thailand and Philippines are among the top Asian countries adopting reneable energy alternatives. Clicking on the country legends on top will hide that country for better filtering allowing for more focused visualization on other countries of interest.

Solar Power (total solar capacity, MW) in South East Asia

The following interactive Line chart displays the total solar power capacity generated per country in Southeast Asia. The purpose of this chart is to visualize just how much energy in mega watts (MW) in total the population can generate using solar energy. From the results, we can see that Vietnam has the highest capacity of generated power using solar energy. Upon hovering on one of the lines, it will focus on that particular country on that particular year highlighted with a distinct colour.

Singapore Renewable Statistics

Renewable Energy Production

This stacked bar chart focueses on the total energy capacity in Megawatts produced by Singapore viaa variety of power sources. We can deduce from the stacked bar chart that Solar Energy is the lowest among the 5 with Biofuel being the highest renewable resource. Hovering over one of the bar chart, the corresponding value associated with that bar chart will be displayed above. To accurately interpret this chart, the height of the bar denotes the percentage contribution to the overall sum of the chart. The shorter the bar height, the less percentage it contributes to the overall.

Renewable Energy Production Doughnut Chart

The doughnut chart is to visualize the stacked bar chart on the left in the form of a doughnut chart which also displays the percentage of each power source. We can see from the dougnut chart solar energy only accounts for 2.8% of the total energy production from Singapore in 2021.

Singapore Solar PV Installation by Region

The interactive map of Singapore shows each state and the planning region they represent including North, South, East, West and Central Singapore together with their capacity, and the total solar panel installations. Hovering over each state will show their respective information and highlights the state.

Singapore Power Consumption Between Industry Areas

The purpose of this chart is to visualize Singapore's overall energy consumption per Industry sector to grasp the idea whether solar energy is reliable in providing power to aid these industries. We can see that in 2021, due to covid lock-down restrictions, the energy consumption is the highest among the 4 areas racking almost 700 Megawatts (MW) of energy. Prior to covid lockdowns, construction takes the most energy consumption at 400 MW of power.

Conclusion

In conclusion, solar energy may be a reliable energy source depending on the country in SouthEast Asia. An in-depth study was conducted on Singapore which showed other alternatives to solar energy yielded greater output in electricity generation capacity, therefore, solar energy may not be the best renewable energy source in Singapore. As compared to Vietnam, it can be observed that Vietnam was able to produce the highest energy capacity using solar compared to other countries making solar energy adoption the best in Vietnam out of the other Southeast Asian countries.
